Life, these days.

Well… the past few months have been heavier than I’d like to admit. A constant grind of stress, of everyday problems stacking faster than I can manage them. Sometimes I catch myself wondering—am I actually capable of holding all of this together, or am I just pretending I am?

I don’t remember the last time I felt genuinely happy. Or even caught myself smiling without thinking. It’s as if I’m living in a permanent state of mourning—though for what, I couldn’t tell you. And the strange part is, it doesn’t feel like depression. Just… emptiness. A kind of quiet apathy. They say apathy is dangerous, that it’s a problem—but I’m not even sure I see it that way anymore.

Before this, there was anger. Not the mild kind—real, consuming fury. The kind that burns through everything and leaves nothing behind but ash.

Now? Just silence.

Things I once loved—photography, films, wandering aimlessly through the world—I barely touch them anymore. They feel distant, like memories from someone else’s life.

Now it’s just worry after worry. An endless saga with no clear turning point.

It’s 1:13 in the morning. Tomorrow is already packed—too many obligations waiting at the door. And here I am, wide awake. Not a trace of sleep. Just thoughts, looping endlessly in the dark.

That’s it.
